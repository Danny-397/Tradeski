SKI — AI FINANCIAL ANALYST
Given today's Fed rate decision, what in my holdings is most exposed?
SKI
Your growth positions carry the most rate sensitivity. When the Fed holds rates elevated, the discount rate applied to future earnings rises — this compresses valuations on high-multiple names first. NVDA and similar tech holdings reprice harder than defensive names. AAPL is more insulated given its cash position and buyback program, though it's not immune. Watch the yield curve spread in your macro ribbon — steepening signals the market expects growth to hold; inversion historically triggers rotation to defensives.
What changed for my positions since yesterday?
SKI
I can see your current holdings and live P&L. For intraday changes, the 1D chart on any position shows exactly where it opened versus where it trades now. Cross-reference that with the news sentiment panel — a shift from neutral to bearish sentiment often precedes price pressure. If a position is down but sentiment is unchanged and macro data is stable, that's more likely noise than a signal worth acting on.
How does current inflation data affect my AAPL and NVDA positions?
SKI
The CPI reading in your macro ribbon is the baseline. Elevated inflation keeps the Fed hawkish, which compresses forward earnings multiples. NVDA gets hit harder — its valuation is weighted toward future AI capex earnings, and a high discount rate makes those earnings worth less today. AAPL is more defensive: its earnings are current, not future-weighted. If CPI cools toward 2%, both names benefit — but NVDA benefits disproportionately as growth multiples expand.
