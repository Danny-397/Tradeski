SKI — AI FINANCIAL ANALYST
Given today's Fed rate decision, what in my holdings is most exposed?
SKI
Your growth positions carry the most rate sensitivity. When the Fed holds rates elevated, the discount rate applied to future earnings rises — this compresses valuations on high-multiple names first. NVDA and similar tech holdings get repriced harder than defensive names. Your AAPL position is more insulated given its cash position and buyback program, though it's not immune. If the Fed signaled a hold today, watch the yield curve spread in the macro ribbon — if it's steepening, that's a signal the market expects growth to hold up; if it's inverting, rotation to defensives is the historical play.
What changed for my positions since yesterday?
SKI
I can see your current holdings and live P&L. For intraday changes, the 1D chart on any position shows you exactly where it opened versus where it trades now. Cross-reference that with the news sentiment panel — a shift from neutral to bearish sentiment often precedes price pressure by a few hours. If a position is down but sentiment is unchanged and macro data is stable, that's more likely noise than a signal worth acting on.
How does current inflation data affect my AAPL and NVDA positions?
SKI
The current CPI reading is in your macro ribbon — use that as the baseline. If inflation is elevated, the Fed stays hawkish, which keeps discount rates high and compresses forward earnings multiples. NVDA gets hit harder here because its valuation is heavily weighted toward future AI capex earnings — a high discount rate makes those future earnings worth less today. AAPL is more defensive: its earnings are current, not future-weighted, and its massive cash position acts as a buffer. If CPI is cooling toward the 2% target, both names benefit — but NVDA benefits disproportionately as growth multiples expand.
